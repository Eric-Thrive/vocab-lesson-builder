# Quick Start Guide

## For Teachers: Creating and Sharing Lessons

### 1. Create a Lesson

1. Open the app
2. Enter vocabulary words (one per line, up to 10)
3. Click "Create Lesson"
4. Wait for AI to generate content and images (~2-3 minutes)

### 2. Share with Students (Two Options)

#### Option A: Quick Share (No Deployment Needed)
- Click "Share Link for Students"
- Copy the link (it will be long)
- Share with students via email, LMS, etc.
- **Note:** Links can be very long and may break in some systems

#### Option B: Short URL (Requires Deployment)
1. Click "Share Link for Students"
2. Click "Download Lesson File for GitHub"
3. Save the JSON file
4. Add it to `public/lessons/` folder in your project
5. Deploy to GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Vercel (see [DEPLOYMENT.md](DEPLOYMENT.md))
6. Share the short URL: `yoursite.com?lesson=animals-lesson`

### 3. Edit Lessons

- Click "Edit" on any definition to modify it
- Click "Edit" on any image to change it (paste URL, upload file, or paste image)
- Changes are saved automatically

### 4. Save Lessons

- Lessons are auto-saved locally in your browser
- Click "Show Saved Lessons" to view all saved lessons
- Export lessons to JSON files for backup or sharing

## For Students: Using Lessons

### Access a Lesson

1. Click the link your teacher shared
2. The lesson loads automatically
3. Start with "Pre-Teaching Vocabulary"

### Navigate the Lesson

1. **Pre-Teaching** - Learn each word with images and examples
   - Use arrow buttons or number buttons to navigate
   - Click "Read the Story" when ready

2. **Story** - Read the story with vocabulary words highlighted
   - Click "Practice Activities" when ready

3. **Practice** - Complete three types of activities:
   - Fill in the Blank
   - Matching (words to pictures)
   - Comprehension questions

### Progress Tracking

- Your answers are saved automatically
- You can close the browser and come back later
- Green checkmarks show completed activities

## Troubleshooting

### Lesson won't load?
- Check your internet connection
- Make sure the URL is complete (not cut off)
- Try refreshing the page

### Images not showing?
- Wait a few seconds for images to load
- Check your internet connection
- Some school networks may block image URLs

### Lost your progress?
- Progress is saved in your browser
- Don't clear browser data/cookies
- Use the same device and browser

## Tips

### For Teachers
- Create lessons in advance and save them
- Test lessons before sharing with students
- Keep lesson files backed up (export to JSON)
- Use descriptive lesson IDs (e.g., "chapter-3-science")

### For Students
- Read through all vocabulary before the story
- Take your time with practice activities
- Ask your teacher if you need help
- Complete all three practice sections
